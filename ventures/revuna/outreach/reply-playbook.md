# Reply playbook — outreach responses, YES flow, and lead tracking

Companion to `cold-email-template.md`, `sample-responses.md`, and `CONCIERGE-SOP.md`. This is what to do the moment a prospect replies to an email or answers a call. Goal of every reply: move to a "yes, send my 3 free replies" or a clean "no" — both are real signal for the 2026-07-23 kill-switch (STRATEGY.md).

## Ground rules (read before drafting any reply)

- **Honest voice, concierge-honest.** The replies are produced by hand today (Jordan + Claude), not by a live automated system — see CONCIERGE-SOP.md. Describe the **service promise** ("nothing sits unanswered, in your voice, you approve before it posts"), never claim automation that doesn't exist yet. Do not imply there's a dashboard, an integration, or posting access. There isn't (Phase 5, deferred).
- **No fabrication.** No invented testimonials, customer counts, star ratings, "other dentists in your area," or urgency ("spots filling up"). We have zero paying customers until we don't — say nothing that isn't true.
- **Pricing is fixed:** Starter **$19/mo** (Google Business Profile). Pro **$39/mo** (+ Yelp + Facebook). No contract, cancel anytime, no setup fee. The offer is **your next 3 replies free, no card needed**, then $19/mo if they want to continue.
- **Keep it short.** These people are busy owner-operators. 3–6 sentences per reply. Match their channel (email → email, text → text).
- **Honor STOP within 10 business days** (CAN-SPAM), realistically same-day. Mark the lead DO-NOT-CONTACT immediately.

### 6. "Not interested" (graceful close + stop)

Then **stop contacting them.** Mark status NO. Do not bump, do not re-pitch. (A "not interested" is a valid kill-switch signal — log it, don't fight it.)

### 7. "STOP / unsubscribe"

Confirm removal in one line, then actually remove them.

> Done — you're removed and won't hear from me again. Sorry for the interruption, and all the best to {business_name}.
>
> Jordan

Then: mark status **DO-NOT-CONTACT** in the tracker (date it), and never email or call this lead again across any batch. This is a hard stop, stronger than a "no."

### 8. No reply after 4–5 business days — one polite bump, then stop

Send **once**, 4–5 business days after the original. Never more than one bump.

> Hi {first_name} — just floating this back up in case it got buried. Still happy to write your next 3 Google review replies free (no card) so you can see if it's useful. If it's not for you, no worries at all and I won't follow up again.

> Jordan

> Reply STOP to opt out of future emails.

If still no reply 4–5 business days after the bump: mark status remains BUMPED → treat as no-signal, **stop**. Don't send a third message.

## The YES flow — onboarding checklist

Trigger: prospect says "yes," "send info," "let's try it," or "sure" on a call or by email/text. Move fast — same day if possible.

### Step 1 — Confirm and collect (one message)

Ask for exactly these three things in a single reply so it's low-effort:

> Great — here's all I need to get your 3 free replies going:
>
> 1. **Your Google Business Profile link** (or just business name + city and I'll find it and confirm with you).
> 2. **How you want to sound** — a couple words is enough: warm/casual, professional, formal? Anything you always want said (or never said)?
> 3. **Who approves the replies before they post** — you, or someone at the front desk? I'll send drafts to whoever signs off.
>
> Send those over and I'll have your first draft back to you quickly.

Capture in the tracker: GBP link, tone/voice notes, approver name + contact.

### Step 2 — Produce the 3 free replies (per CONCIERGE-SOP)

1. Get the Google Business Profile link; if they don't have it, search "{business name} {city} google maps" and confirm the right listing with them.
2. Have them copy the text of up to 3 unanswered reviews (text/email — whatever's easiest), **or** read them off their public GBP.
3. Paste each review into a Claude session and draft a reply against the tone bar in `sample-responses.md`: specific (never generic "thanks for your feedback"), owner's voice, under 80 words, never defensive, always invite offline resolution for complaints.
4. Send the drafts to the approver. They approve/edit.
5. **The customer pastes each approved reply into their own Google Business Profile reply box and posts it** — Revuna has no posting access yet (Phase 5, deferred). Be clear about this; it's the honest current process.

### Step 3 — The conversion moment (after the 3rd free reply)

Once all 3 are delivered and posted, convert:

> That's your 3 — hope they hit the mark and saved you the hassle. If you'd like me to keep handling every new review the same way, it's $19/mo, no contract, cancel anytime. Want me to keep going?

If **yes to paid**: collect payment. **Stripe Payment Link is not set up yet** (ROADMAP.md Phase 4 — created only once 3+ customers are paying). Until then, per CONCIERGE-SOP, collect via Venmo / Cash App / Zelle:

> `[STRIPE PAYMENT LINK — Phase 4, pending]` — until that's live: "Easiest for now is Venmo/Zelle/Cash App at {handle} — $19 and I'll keep every new review covered. I'll switch to a proper card link once a few of you are on board."

Log the conversion, mark status **YES**, and note payment method + date. Hitting **3 paying customers** is the trigger to stand up Stripe (Phase 4) and start Google OAuth verification (Phase 5) per CONCIERGE-SOP.

If **not yet**: "No worries — the door's open at $19 whenever. Thanks for giving it a shot." Mark as REPLIED (warm), not NO, unless they clearly decline for good.
